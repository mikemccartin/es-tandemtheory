## Source Analysis Summary

The English source is a single-page marketing site for Tandem Theory, a marketing and technology company based in the US. The site follows a modern agency structure: Hero -> About/Humans -> Technology Suite -> Services/Capabilities -> Results/Testimonials -> Contact CTA -> Footer.

**Brand voice**: Confident, data-driven, warm, relationship-oriented. Uses short punchy headlines contrasted with explanatory body copy. Emphasis on the "Humans + Technology" equation as core positioning.

**Cultural friction points identified**:
- "Full-Stack Marketing" -- tech jargon that maps to "marketing integral" in Mexican agency language
- "Proof, Not Promises" -- English parallelism that needed cultural adaptation
- "Let's Talk" -- casual CTA that needed warmth calibration
- Testimonials from English-speaking executives -- required transcreation notation
- "off-the-shelf" idiom -- no direct equivalent
- "$1.2B" -- kept as USD since these are US results

## Key Creative Decisions

| Section | English (source) | Spanish (transcreated) | Decision & Rationale |
|---------|-----------------|----------------------|---------------------|
| Hero headline | "Proven Experts. Powerful Technology. Exceptional Growth." | "Expertos probados. Tecnologia poderosa. Crecimiento excepcional." | Close adaptation -- the three-beat structure works in both languages. "Probados" conveys earned credibility. |
| Hero CTA | "Let's Talk" | "Hablemos" | Direct, warm, universally understood in Mexico. Preferred over "Platiquemos" (too casual for B2B) or "Conversemos" (too distant). |
| Results headline | "Proof, Not Promises" | "Hechos, no promesas" | Punchier parallel structure. "Hechos" (facts/deeds) carries the Mexican cultural value of "actions speak louder than words" more effectively than a literal "Pruebas, no promesas". |
| Services headline | "Full-Stack Marketing Capability" | "Marketing integral de principio a fin" | "Marketing integral" is standard Mexican agency positioning language. "De principio a fin" reinforces full-service without the tech jargon of "full-stack". |
| Services subtitle | "The strategic thinking of a top consultancy. The creative firepower..." | "El pensamiento estrategico de una consultoria de primer nivel. La fuerza creativa..." | "Firepower" -> "fuerza creativa" avoids the military metaphor which doesn't resonate the same way in Mexican business culture. |
| CTA "Interested?" | "Interested?" | "¿Le interesa?" | Uses usted form for the formal body copy context. The inverted question mark is mandatory. |
| CTA offer | "Tell us your top three problems. We'll solve one for free." | "Cuentenos sus tres principales desafios. Resolvemos uno sin costo." | "Desafios" (challenges) is more diplomatic than "problemas" (problems) in Mexican business culture, where directness about problems can feel confrontational. "Sin costo" is more natural than "gratis" in B2B context. |
| Nav "Humans" | "Humans" | "Nosotros" | "Nosotros" (About us) is the standard Mexican agency navigation label for this type of section. "Humanos" as a nav label would sound odd. |
| Tech tagline | "Platform agnostic. Intelligence obsessed. Results guaranteed." | "Agnosticos de plataforma. Obsesionados con la inteligencia. Resultados garantizados." | Three-beat structure preserved. Natural flow in Spanish. |
| About body | "Our founders spent decades..." | "Nuestros fundadores pasaron decadas..." | Uses simple past tense ("pasaron" not "han pasado") per Mexican Spanish preference. |
| Hiring CTA | "Join Our Team - View Open Positions" | "Unete a nuestro equipo - Ver posiciones abiertas" | Uses tu form for the CTA (more dynamic, inviting), consistent with the agent definition's guidance on CTA register. |
| Privacy Policy | "Privacy Policy" | "Aviso de privacidad" | Standard Mexican legal terminology. "Politica de privacidad" is acceptable but "Aviso de privacidad" is the legally required term in Mexico per LFPDPPP. |

## Pronoun Strategy

| Context | Choice | Rationale |
|---------|--------|-----------|
| Body copy, descriptions | Usted | Professional warmth appropriate for B2B decision-makers (CMOs, VPs, directors). Safe default for presidential-level copy. |
| CTAs and headlines | Tu (selectively) | "Hablemos" and "Unete" use imperative forms that feel dynamic and inviting without being overly formal. |
| Company voice | Nosotros | Inclusive, team-oriented. Standard for Mexican agency self-description. |
| Plural address | Ustedes (implied) | Never vosotros. |

## Testimonial Handling

All three testimonials were transcreated (not directly translated) to read naturally in Mexican Spanish. Each attribution includes a subtle note: "(Transcreacion del testimonio original en ingles)" to maintain transparency that these are adapted quotes from English-speaking executives, not original Spanish quotes.

Key adaptations:
- **Sarah Philips**: "inside and out" -> "por dentro y por fuera" (natural Mexican expression)
- **Sara Kamber**: "extension of my own team" -> "extension de mi propio equipo" (this metaphor translates well and resonates strongly in Mexican relationship-first business culture)
- **Christopher Houston**: "no-brainer" -> "la decision mas obvia" (no direct equivalent for "no-brainer" in Spanish; this captures the ease-of-decision intent)

## Flagged Items for Review

1. **"$1.2B+" stat**: Kept in USD format since these are US business results. If targeting Mexico specifically, consider adding "USD" clarification or converting to "mil millones de dolares" for clarity (since "billon" in Spanish means trillion).
2. **Testimonial transcreation notes**: The small italic disclaimers below each testimonial may or may not be desired by the client. They add transparency but also visual noise. Recommend reviewing with stakeholders.
3. **Footer "Aviso de privacidad"**: This links to privacy-policy.html which presumably is still in English. A Spanish privacy policy page would be needed for a fully localized experience.
4. **Hiring CTA**: Links to Paycom English careers page. A localized careers page or Spanish application process may be needed if targeting Mexican applicants.
5. **"7.5M+" customers stat**: This number format works in Mexico (same decimal/comma convention as US). No adaptation needed.
